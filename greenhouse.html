Greenhouse Gases

The Problem

Human activities like burning fossil fuels, land clearing, and agriculture are increasing greenhouse gas concentrations in the world, leading to global warming.

What the EPA is Doing

From its fuel consumption and energy use, the EPA is monitoring emissions. By 2020, it’s goal is to reduce emissions by 25%. Through several programs like GreenChill, over 345 million metric tons of greenhouse gases were reduced by the EPA’s partners in 2010. This is equivalent to the emissions from 81 million vehicles. Also, regulatory initiatives were developed by the EPA to increase efficiency and reduce emissions. An example is the vehicle greenhouse gas rules, which will save consumers about $1.7 trillion by 2025, at the gas pump. The Clean Power Plan addresses emissions caused by power plants. In 2030, this plan will be fully in place, pollution in the power sector will be about 32% below the 2005 levels.

5 Things You Can Do

  1. Plant-based Diets: Eat more local veggies! Meat production, processing, and transportation release tons of CO2 into the atmosphere.
  2. Drive Smart and Drive Less: Bike, take public transportation, or walk. One gallon of gas is equivalent to 20 pounds of CO2.
  3. Use Energy-Efficient Products: Set your appliances to energy saver mode. Fluorescent bulbs, for example, consume less energy than standard incandescent bulbs.
  4. Plant a Tree: Trees absorb CO2 and produce oxygen. One tree can absorb around one ton of CO2 in its lifetime.
  5. Raise Awareness: Encourage your friends and family to make changes in their lives. Work with your public officials to create policies and programs that are climate-forward
