Deforestation

The Problem

The biggest threat to forests, globally, is deforestation. When forests are converted to agriculture, road construction, and other non-forest uses, deforestation occurs.

What Organizations are Doing

Organizations like the Forest Stewardship Council are offering independent certifications for products that are made from paper, fiber, and recycled pulp, to help consumers find forest-friendly products. Also, Greenpeace is pressuring corporations, successfully, to get rid of unsustainable suppliers from their supply chains. For example, in 2010, Burger King stopped using the Indonesian conglomerate Sinar Mars. Several other organizations like the Amazon Watch, are working to protect indigenous communities in the Amazon Rainforest. The World Wildlife Fund and the Sierra Club are seeking to protect forests around the world. The Environmental Defense Fund is pushing for bills that provide landowners with financial incentives to conserve land.

5 Things You Can Do

  1. Plant Trees!
  2. Go Paperless: Refuse reciepts and ask your bank for electronic statements
  3. Buy Recycled Products
  4. Plant-Based Diets: Eat more local veggies.
  5. Embrace ecological agriculture and sustainable urban farms
